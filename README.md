## Fundamentals of Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs)
## 1. Introduction

Generative Artificial Intelligence (Generative AI) refers to systems that can generate new content such as text, images, audio, and code by learning patterns from existing data. Unlike traditional AI models that focus on classification or prediction, generative models aim to create new, meaningful outputs.

Large Language Models (LLMs) are a major breakthrough in this field. They are capable of understanding context, generating human-like text, and performing tasks such as translation, summarization, and coding.

## 2.1 Probability and Data Distribution

Generative AI models learn the probability distribution of data:

Learn patterns from training data

## 2.2 Types of Generative Models
Autoregressive Models – Generate data step-by-step
Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) – Learn compressed representations
Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) – Compete between generator and discriminator
Diffusion Models – Generate data by removing noise
Transformers – Most advanced and widely used
